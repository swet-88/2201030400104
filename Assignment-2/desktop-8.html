Virupaksha Temple
Popular for being an active temple since the 7th century, Virupaksha Temple is located on the southern side of river Tungabhadra, and is an important pilgrimage centre for Shiva devotees.

Hampi Bazaar
Once a thriving market place bordered by living quarters of the erstwhile nobles, the Hampi Bazaar extends from the Virupaksha Temple to the foot of the Matunga Hill.

Vittala Temple
Dedicated to Lord Vishnu and home to the fascinating musical pillars and the incomparable stone chariot, the Vittala Temple complex is a major attraction in the city.

Coracle Ride
The round cane boats called coracles, have been an important source of transportation in the city since the 15th century. It is a must-do activity in Hampi!

Sasivekalu Ganesha Temple
The Sasivekalu Ganesha Temple houses an eight feet tall statue of Lord Ganesha. The huge statue is carved out of a single rock.

Pattabhirama Temple
Located about 15 minutes away from the Hampi ruins, the glorious Pattabhirama Temple is dedicated to Lord Rama and is known for its complex architecture.
