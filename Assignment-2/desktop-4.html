Jodhpur
Known for the magnificent Mehrangarh Fort, famous palaces and lakes, Jodhpur is a popular tourist destination, divided into the Old City and New City by a 10 km long wall.

Mehrangarh Fort
Built by the Rathore clan of Jodhpur in 1460, the well-presrved Mehrangarh Fort towers above 400 feet, overlooking the city.

Umaid Bhawan Palace
Built by Maharaja Umaid Singh, the palace was made for the welfare of the common people at a time of severe drought. It was converted to a heritage hotel in 1977.

Mandore Garden
Located near the old capital of the Rathores, Mandore Garden is home to splendid architectural gems. It is about 30 minutes from Jodhpur city.

Jaswant Thada
Made in memory of Maharaja Jaswant Singh, the white-marble building is minutes from Mehrangarh Fort, and depicts lovely sculptures, beautiful domes and a striking peacock memorial.

Clock Tower Market
A vibrant city-square within the old city, the market is also known as Sardar Market, named after the Marwar ruler who commissioned the striking Clock Tower.

Khejarla Fort
Khejarla Fort is a 400-year-old heritage fort located approximately 85 kilometres from Jodhpur.
